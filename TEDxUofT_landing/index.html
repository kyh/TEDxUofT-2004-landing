A little about TED

About TED

TED is a nonprofit organization devoted to Ideas Worth Spreading. Started as a conference in California 26 years ago, TED has grown to support those world-changing ideas with many initiatives.


At a TED conference, the world’s leading thinkers and doers are asked to give the talk of their lives in 18 minutes or less. TED speakers have included Roger Ebert, Sheryl Sandberg, Bill Gates, Elizabeth Gilbert, Benoit Mandelbrot, Philippe Starck, Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala, Brian Greene, Isabel Allende and former UK Prime Minister Gordon Brown. Three major TED events are held each year: The TED Conference takes place every spring in Vancouver, Canada, simultaneous with TEDActive, in Whistler, BC; and the TEDGlobal Conference takes place each summer in Edinburgh, Scotland.


On TED.com, talks from TED conferences are shared with the world for free as TED Talks videos. A new TED Talk is posted every weekday. Through the Open Translation Project, TED Talks are subtitled by volunteers worldwide into more than 90 languages. Through our distribution networks, TED Talks are shared on TV, radio, Netflix and many websites.

About TEDx

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TEDTalks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized. (Subject to certain rules and regulations.)

We're currently recruiting organizers for the 2014 event
If you enjoy TED, TEDx and believe in ‘ideas worth spreading’ then we encourage you to apply!

Executive assistant

Given this positions dynamic nature, you’ll be able to work in various aspects of the conference. As the executive assistant you’ll provide support as needed to the executive team in particular to project management and logistics

Social Media Specialist

We have experience immense growth in social media, so much so that we think it is about time we had someone purely concentrate their efforts on maintaining a constant flow of content to our social media outlets. Proof of strong social media presence must be shown as will a writing sample.

Campus Ambassadors

We want to make sure we are clear that TEDxUofT is a tri-campus event, and we want to make sure we reach as wide an audience as possible. We are looking for outgoing, well-connected, active individuals within their perspective campuses who are proud to be part of the huge UofT community.

Funds Manager

It is pretty important that someone makes sure our finances are in order. As the funds manager you will maintain an updated record of finances, and work closely to secure external sources of funding i.e. university grants and sponsorship opportunities.

Junior Designer/Developer

We have some pretty incredible designers on board, but there is plenty of work to go around. You may have an opportunity to work in the designing of various event materials from infographs to websites to brand mockups. Let your imagination run wild.

After Party Coordinator

We are looking to incorporate an afterparty component to the conference experience. The afterparty will continue the conversation of the conference, and will be highly interactive. This is an amazing opportunity for highly artistic, creative and social individual who is looking for a challenge

Video Production Associate

We would love to have someone capture exactly what it takes to put a TEDx event together in video form, from drawing storyboards to filming commercials and editing videos. Knowledge of professional editing software is required.
